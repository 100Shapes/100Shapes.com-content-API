What is interactive video?

Interactive videos blend video content with user interaction. Visual elements within a video invite viewers to click or tap to perform an action. They can be associated with items in the story, triggered by the timeline or are continually visible as an overlay to the video. Be it gestures, voice, touch or clicks — the richness of the user interaction can run across the spectrum. Interactive videos create multi-layered, immersive, user-driven experiences.

Benefits of interactive videos

Deepen the experience

Incorporate interactive archive content into a documentary or surface additional clips and audio in your drama to enrich your narrative.

Hand over control to the audience

Let your viewers find their own way. Allow them to follow a character, choose their camera perspective or even change the ending.

Make it memorable

Keeping your viewers active will hold their attention for longer, increase engagement, recall, satisfaction and time spent watching a video.

Understand your audience

Identify what makes your audience tick. Track your viewers’ interactions to determine what grabs their attention and how your video is performing.

Use cases of interactive video

Change how we learn

Transform video learning into a participatory experience that can be personalised based on the needs of a learner.

Transform music performances

Hand over the reigns to your audience. Let them watch from backstage or the mosh pit, single out a vocal or switch tracks.

Gamify your story

Let your audience fight alongside the hero or choose their own path. Inject a thrilling sense of jeopardy with interactive triggers and content, blended seamlessly with high-quality video.

Let your audience buy what they see

Use interactive video to drive direct sales by allowing users to buy what is shown.

Designing an interactive video

Whether you are selling a product through an interactive video or creating the next cutting edge immersive drama, accomplishing your goals starts with a clear understanding of your objectives and what makes your audience tick. Our process typically includes:

1. A clear view of where you are

In order to take your product or service forward, you need to know where you are to begin with. We’ll kick things off by gathering information about your business and users.

2. Establishing your vision

Next it’s time to work out where you want to be. Armed with the information we’ve gathered, we brainstorm and prioritise strategies that have a measurable impact on your business.

3. Get the job done

With a clear road map of who does what and when, it’s time to roll up our sleeves and implement your vision. Our strategists and designers will create moodboards, wireframes, content maps and designs that detail your entire video experience.

4. Incorporating content

Content is king! Content and design streams run concurrently to allow them to feed into one another. We work with your team to create a detailed content map that plots exactly where your interactive material features throughout. This process not only helps inform how the video content is scripted, it also determines how the design is presented to your audience.

Once your project is out in the world, we’ll gather metrics and useful feedback to determine its success. Armed with these insights we’ll make any necessary improvements before tackling the next objective.

Case Study

Run the Jewels

Exclusive access to the masterful hip-hop supremos Run the Jewels

An interactive music documentary that gives exclusive access to US hip-hop duo Run The Jewels. This interactive video gives audiences the best spots at their sell-out London gig and the chance to hang out with the guys backstage.

Decide which band member you want to speak to, where you want to hang out, even where to watch the gig (this means you can be right at the front without getting crushed or sweaty!).

This interactive film gets the viewer as close as possible to the band all the while being a lively and fun documentary.

We wanted the viewers to be the ones who call the shots and give them the opportunity to spend time with their favourite artists in the way they choose. No middlemen and no presenters.

View it at BBC Taster- Run the Jewels

Case Study

Footballers Utd

The powerful story of Scottish footballers in World War One

Footballers United is a unique interactive online drama — a narrative with an accompanying archive of images, text and video — set in Edinburgh during World War One and revolving around two young men and two young women as they cope with their new reality.

Viewers are presented with an array of archive content, from photos to original video, that support and enliven the story. A clever interactive timeline prompts the audience to access related archive content and cleverly knits it all together.

For a more personal experience, you can sign in via Facebook to map the events to your own network of friends, showing how your friends would have fared throughout the war.

Launched in December 2014, Footballers United is an iWonder commission for the BBC World War One centenary season, produced by Somethin’ Else, user experience and design lead by 100 Shapes.

This interactive drama blends high quality video with archived content in a way we’ve never done before; which not only gives the story depth but brings an important topic like WW1 to a new and contemporary audience.
