What is a Heuristic evaluation

A usability specialist will conduct a thorough audit of your website or app, benchmarking each page or feature for fitness, based on best-practice usability heuristics ('rules of thumb'). Some of the factors we consider are:

  • Navigation
  • Information architecture
  • Layout
  • Legibility
  • Interaction
  • Visual consistency
  • Signposting and calls to action
  • Use of imagery

Each feature is given ratings based on a simple ‘traffic light’ system:

Red - Urgent action required. A serious issue that is likely to be a pain-point to users, directly affecting exit/bounce rates and conversion.

Amber - Simple fix or consideration for improvement. A small problem with a quick and obvious solution. Or, a larger issue with only a moderate impact on usability.

Green - No action required. A feature that works well and should be retained.

You will receive a detailed report, which includes explanations for each rating, and written recommendations for 'quick fix' solutions, where appropriate. This can be a valuable reference for future development.

When to consider heuristic evaluation

Heuristic evaluation is perfect if you're considering making some 'quick fix' improvements to your product or you want to bring it in line with usability best-practice.

Heuristic evaluation is most useful when combined with insights into actual users - perhaps you've collected customer feedback or have seen interesting behaviour in website analytics, or maybe you've done some usability testing in the past? It's always much better to approach a heuristic review with specific areas of focus in mind.

On its own, heuristic evaluation is limited. Even an experienced usability specialist can't predict exactly how a user in the real world might behave, particularly if the product has a specific audience. Usability testing is the best, possible way to evaluate how real users behave and react.

Heuristic review is also limited to the evaluating the product you already have. If you're planning an expansion or major re-design, you should consider audience research, and also usability testing as part of the design process.
