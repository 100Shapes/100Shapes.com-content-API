What is a User Experience Strategy?

UX strategy is about the big picture. It’s a long-term business strategy to improve, measure and manage the user experience across multiple channels and devices.

It provides your team with a clear understanding of how your business needs to serve its customers and how to harness technology to do so.

Approached at the organisational level, it will help you prioritise objectives, develop a clear roadmap of improvement for user experience and help senior stakeholders understand the case for investment.

Creating a clear UX strategy

Defining your user experience strategy has many parallels to how you define your business strategy. It comprises of the following steps:

1. A clear view of where you are

In order to take your product or service forward, you need to know where you are to begin with. We’ll kick things off by gathering information about your business and users.

2. Establishing your vision

Next it’s time to work out where you want to be. Armed with the information we’ve gathered, we brainstorm and prioritise strategies that have a measurable impact on your business.

3. Get the job done

With a clear road map of who does what and when, it’s time to roll up our sleeves and implement your vision. Our strategists and designers work with you to develop your goals into a tangible product (or service) to deliver the business outcomes you are targeting.

4. Time to respond

Once your project is out in the world, we’ll gather metrics and useful feedback to determine its success. With these insights we’ll make any necessary improvements before tackling the next objective.

Case Study

London Live

A digital platfrom for London

London Live is a British television channel, launched at 6:30pm on 31st March 2014. Combining London news and entertainment, it is broadcast on Freeview channel 8, satellite, cable and online.

When 100 Shapes were commissioned to design the London Live website we assembled a team of user researchers, user experience designers and information architects to deliver the project with a user-centered approach. Up-front in order to identify genuine user needs, we ran an in-depth audience research study with 10 participants based on a target demographic.

The study examined the potential opportunities to fit into the lives of the audience members by understanding how well they were served for certain types of information such as news, travel, weather and entertainment.

By understanding where participants were under-served and comparing that to what was important we were able to identify the biggest opportunities for London Live and inform the design direction.
